The Life of Stars

Stars are born in vast clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Over time, gravity pulls these materials together to form a star. Here's their life cycle:

  1. Birth: Stars form in nebulae from collapsing gas and dust.
  2. Main Sequence: Stars like our Sun spend most of their lives fusing hydrogen into helium.
  3. Red Giant or Supergiant: When hydrogen runs out, the star expands and cools.
  4. Death: Small stars become white dwarfs, while massive stars explode as supernovae, leaving neutron stars or black holes.
